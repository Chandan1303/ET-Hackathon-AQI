NATIONAL AMBIENT AIR QUALITY STANDARDS (NAAQS)
=========================================

Pollutant Standards for India (µg/m³):

PM2.5 (Fine Particulate Matter):
- Annual Average: 40 µg/m³
- 24-hour Average: 60 µg/m³
- Health Advisory: Levels above 150 µg/m³ require immediate action

PM10 (Respirable Particulate Matter):
- Annual Average: 60 µg/m³
- 24-hour Average: 100 µg/m³

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO2):
- Annual Average: 40 µg/m³
- 24-hour Average: 80 µg/m³

Sulfur Dioxide (SO2):
- Annual Average: 50 µg/m³
- 24-hour Average: 80 µg/m³

Carbon Monoxide (CO):
- 8-hour Average: 2 mg/m³
- 1-hour Average: 4 mg/m³

Ozone (O3):
- 8-hour Average: 100 µg/m³
- 1-hour Average: 180 µg/m³

Lead (Pb):
- Annual Average: 0.5 µg/m³
- Quarterly Average: 1.0 µg/m³

Air Quality Index (AQI) Categories:
- Good (0-50): Minimal impact
- Satisfactory (51-100): Minor breathing discomfort to sensitive people
- Moderate (101-200): Breathing discomfort to people with lung, heart disease
- Poor (201-300): Breathing discomfort to most people on prolonged exposure
- Very Poor (301-400): Respiratory illness on prolonged exposure
- Severe (401-500): Affects healthy people and seriously impacts those with existing diseases

Emergency Response Thresholds:
- AQI > 300: Ban on construction activities, brick kilns
- AQI > 400: Graded Response Action Plan (GRAP) Stage 4 implementation
- PM2.5 > 250 µg/m³: School closures and work-from-home advisories

Monitoring Requirements:
- Continuous Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Stations (CAAQMS) required every 10 km in urban areas
- Real-time data reporting every 15 minutes
- Calibration and quality assurance quarterly

Health Impact Guidelines:
- Sensitive groups include children, elderly, pregnant women, and those with respiratory/cardiac conditions
- Outdoor activities should be limited when AQI exceeds 200
- Indoor air purification recommended when PM2.5 exceeds 100 µg/m³

Industrial Emission Standards:
- Thermal power plants: Particulate matter < 30 mg/Nm³
- Cement plants: PM < 30 mg/Nm³, SO2 < 200 mg/Nm³
- Iron & Steel: PM < 50 mg/Nm³
- Vehicle emissions: BS-VI standards mandatory in all urban areas
