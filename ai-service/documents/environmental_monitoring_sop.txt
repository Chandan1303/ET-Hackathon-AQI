STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP-ENV-001)
CONTINUOUS AMBIENT AIR QUALITY MONITORING STATIONS (CAAQMS)
=============================================================

PURPOSE:
Establish standardized procedures for operating and maintaining Continuous Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Stations to ensure accurate, reliable, and consistent air quality data collection.

SCOPE:
Applies to all CAAQMS operators, technicians, and environmental officers responsible for air quality monitoring across urban and industrial zones.

EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATIONS:
- PM2.5 & PM10: Beta Attenuation Monitor (BAM) or Tapered Element Oscillating Microbalance (TEOM)
- SO2: UV Fluorescence Method
- NO2: Chemiluminescence Method
- CO: Non-Dispersive Infrared (NDIR) Method
- O3: UV Photometric Method
- Meteorological Sensors: Temperature, Humidity, Wind Speed/Direction, Barometric Pressure

CALIBRATION REQUIREMENTS:
Daily:
- Zero and span calibration checks
- Verification of data logger time synchronization
- Visual inspection of inlet filters and sampling lines

Weekly:
- Multipoint calibration verification
- Flow rate validation (±5% tolerance)
- PM sampling head cleaning

Monthly:
- Full calibration using certified reference gases
- Leak test of sampling manifold
- Meteorological sensor verification against reference instruments

Quarterly:
- Performance audit by third-party agency
- Replacement of critical consumables (filters, tubing)
- Data quality assurance report submission

ROUTINE OPERATIONS:
1. Morning Checklist (08:00 hours):
   - Check power supply and UPS status
   - Verify real-time data transmission to central server
   - Inspect physical integrity of monitoring shelter
   - Record ambient conditions in logbook

2. Data Verification (12:00 hours):
   - Review live data trends for anomalies
   - Flag invalid data points (calibration periods, power failures)
   - Cross-check with neighboring stations for consistency

3. Evening Checklist (18:00 hours):
   - Download backup data to local storage
   - Generate preliminary 24-hour average reports
   - Communicate exceedances to control room

ALARM THRESHOLDS:
- PM2.5 > 150 µg/m³: Issue "Very Poor" advisory
- PM2.5 > 250 µg/m³: Trigger GRAP Stage III protocols
- PM10 > 350 µg/m³: Alert industrial sources for emission control
- SO2 > 200 µg/m³: Immediate industrial stack inspection
- NO2 > 180 µg/m³: Traffic management review required

TROUBLESHOOTING:
Symptom: Negative PM readings
- Cause: Inlet filter clogging or flow rate deviation
- Action: Replace inlet filter, verify pump operation

Symptom: Erratic gas analyzer readings
- Cause: Zero drift or span shift
- Action: Perform immediate calibration, check for leaks

Symptom: Data transmission failure
- Cause: Network connectivity or modem malfunction
- Action: Restart data logger, verify SIM card balance, check router

Symptom: High baseline drift in SO2/NO2
- Cause: Contaminated optical cells
- Action: Clean reaction chamber, replace zero air filter

EMERGENCY PROCEDURES:
Power Failure:
- UPS should provide minimum 4 hours backup
- Switch to manual sampling mode if prolonged outage expected
- Log downtime duration and reason

Extreme Weather:
- Secure monitoring shelter during high winds
- Elevate equipment during flood warnings
- Install temporary shielding during dust storms

Equipment Malfunction:
- Switch to redundant analyzer if available
- Notify central control room within 30 minutes
- Arrange emergency maintenance within 4 hours

QUALITY ASSURANCE/QUALITY CONTROL (QA/QC):
- Data capture target: >90% uptime per month
- Precision: Coefficient of variation <5% for replicate measurements
- Accuracy: ±10% of reference method
- Detection limits as per CPCB guidelines
- Annual inter-laboratory comparison mandatory

DATA MANAGEMENT:
- Raw data archived for minimum 10 years
- 15-minute averages calculated and transmitted
- Hourly, daily, monthly summaries auto-generated
- Annual data validation and reporting
- Public dashboard updated every 15 minutes

PERSONNEL REQUIREMENTS:
- Station Operator: Diploma in Environmental Science/Electronics
- Maintenance Technician: ITI certificate in Electronics/Instrumentation
- QA Officer: Degree in Environmental Engineering
- Training: 40 hours initial + 16 hours annual refresher

REPORTING:
- Real-time data: Continuous to CPCB/SPCB servers
- Daily summary: Email by 10:00 hours next day
- Monthly report: Submitted by 5th of following month
- Annual compliance report: By January 31st
- Immediate incident reports: Within 1 hour of detection

REGULATORY COMPLIANCE:
- Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) Guidelines 2015
- National Ambient Air Quality Monitoring Network (NAAM) protocols
- ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System
- Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) specifications
