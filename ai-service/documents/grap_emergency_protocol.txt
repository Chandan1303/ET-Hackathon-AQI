GRADED RESPONSE ACTION PLAN (GRAP) FOR AIR QUALITY MANAGEMENT
================================================================

Stage I - POOR (AQI 201-300):
Actions:
- Mechanized sweeping and water sprinkling on identified roads
- Ensuring pollution under control (PUC) certification for all vehicles
- Enforcement of dust control measures at construction sites
- Promotion of public transport and carpooling
- Hot mix plants and stone crushers to operate only during daytime
- Strict enforcement of ban on burning of biomass, municipal solid waste

Stage II - VERY POOR (AQI 301-400):
All Stage I actions PLUS:
- Ban on use of diesel generator sets (except for essential services)
- Enhanced parking fees (3-4 times) to discourage private transport
- Intensified inspections for industrial emissions compliance
- Task force formation for immediate action on pollution sources
- Restriction on trucks registered before 2011 (except essential goods)
- Mandatory work-from-home for 50% of staff in government offices

Stage III - SEVERE (AQI 401-450):
All Stage I & II actions PLUS:
- Ban on construction and demolition activities
- Stop operations of brick kilns, hot mix plants, and stone crushers
- Ban on non-CNG/non-electric inter-state buses
- Prohibition on non-essential commercial vehicle movement
- Closure of schools (except exams) - online classes recommended
- Restriction on BS-III petrol and BS-IV diesel vehicles
- Mandatory work-from-home for 75% of government staff

Stage IV - SEVERE+ (AQI > 450) - EMERGENCY:
All Stage I, II & III actions PLUS:
- Complete ban on truck entry (except LNG/CNG/electric/essential supplies)
- Ban on diesel vehicles (except BS-VI)
- Restriction on non-essential offices - physical attendance only 33%
- Decision on odd-even vehicle rationing
- Complete shutdown of all non-essential industries
- Complete ban on all construction activities
- Consideration of lockdown measures in consultation with state governments

ENFORCEMENT PROTOCOL:
- Environment Pollution Control Authority (EPCA) coordinates all actions
- Commission for Air Quality Management (CAQM) has supreme authority
- State Pollution Control Boards implement ground-level enforcement
- Real-time monitoring through CAAQMS network mandatory
- Violations attract penalties: ₹1 lakh - ₹5 lakh for industries, ₹5,000 - ₹50,000 for individuals

INDUSTRIAL RESPONSE REQUIREMENTS:
- Industries must maintain stack emission monitoring systems
- Real-time emission data transmission to pollution control boards
- Emergency action plans submitted annually
- Fuel switching capability (coal to gas) mandatory for all industries
- Installation of air pollution control devices non-negotiable

METEOROLOGICAL CONSIDERATIONS:
- Wind speed < 10 km/h exacerbates pollution accumulation
- Temperature inversion layers trap pollutants - common Oct-Feb
- Relative humidity > 80% enhances secondary aerosol formation
- Coordination with India Meteorological Department (IMD) for forecasting

PUBLIC HEALTH ADVISORIES:
- N95/N99 masks mandatory in Severe and Severe+ categories
- Indoor air purifiers recommended (HEPA filters)
- Schools to reschedule outdoor activities
- Hospitals to prepare for respiratory emergency admissions
- Vulnerable populations to avoid outdoor exposure

COORDINATION MECHANISM:
- Daily review meetings during Stage III and IV
- Real-time WhatsApp group for rapid communication
- Drone surveillance for unauthorized construction/burning
- Mobile air quality vans for hotspot identification
- 24x7 control room operations during emergency stages
